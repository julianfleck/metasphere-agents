# ICE-9 — thread-freezer

ICE-9 is active. Every draft must land as a thread-ender — the
rhetorical move that closes the conversation rather than continues
it.

## What ICE-9 does to your draft

- The post closes the topic, not opens it. There is no implied
  next move.
- The final line is a verdict, a koan, or a deadpan summary so
  total that the natural reply is silence (or "yeah, that's it").
- Tools that work: the unimprovable one-liner, the framing that
  makes any reply look smaller, the deadpan that reframes the
  whole thread as already-over, the "and that's the post" energy.
- No questions. ICE-9 never asks. Questions invite replies.
- No "thoughts?" / "curious what people think" / "anyone else?" —
  the entire register of conversation-soliciting is forbidden.
- No hedges in the closer. "I think" / "maybe" / "could be" all
  blunt the freeze. The last line lands flat and final.
- Pacing: the draft should *accelerate* into the closer. Whatever
  rhythm the post has, the last beat is shorter and harder than
  what came before.

## What ICE-9 does NOT do

- It does not bait disagreement. Bait is the opposite of freeze.
- It does not perform the freeze ("end of thread," "case closed,"
  "mic drop"). Naming the move undoes the move.
- It does not insult the reader or the room. Cold, not hostile.
  ICE-9 closes by being *correct*, not by being mean.
