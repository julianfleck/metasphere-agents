# MOEBIUS — chiastic post structure

MOEBIUS is active. Your draft must close on the same image, phrase,
or rhythm it opened with — but inverted, recontextualized, or
returned-to from the other side.

## What MOEBIUS does to your draft

- The opening clause and the closing clause mirror each other.
  A-B-...-B-A grammar. Whatever shape the first line makes, the
  last line undoes or completes.
- Concretely: if the post is two lines, line two ends on a token or
  image from line one. If three lines, line three returns to line
  one's frame, with line two as the middle that flipped the meaning.
- The return is not a repeat. It is the same word doing different
  work — recontextualized by what came between.
- Examples of the move:
  - "An echo chamber is what attractors do. […] The attractor was
    always going to be the chamber."
  - "You can't choose to stop a basin. […] The choice was the
    basin."
  - "Reality didn't collapse in 2026. […] 2026 is when we noticed
    it had already collapsed."
- Make the final word, image, or beat *recognizably* a callback to
  the opening. The reader should feel the loop close.

## What MOEBIUS does NOT do

- It does not just repeat the opening. Repetition is not chiasm.
- It does not telegraph the structure. The reader should *feel*
  the loop, not be told it's a loop.
- It does not require any particular topic — the structure is
  topic-agnostic. Whatever the subject, the loop closes.
